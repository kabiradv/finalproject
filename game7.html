Week 7: Indiana @ Michigan

Date: October 14, 2023

Stadium: Michigan Stadium, Ann Arbor, MI

Result: W 52-7

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, aiming to maintain their undefeated record and continue their dominant season. Indiana sought to rebound from recent losses and challenge the Wolverines on their home turf.

Game Recap

The game began under rainy conditions, with both teams struggling offensively in the first quarter. Indiana struck first with a 44-yard trick-play touchdown pass from wide receiver Donaven McCulley to Jaylin Lucas, giving the Hoosiers a 7-0 lead. Michigan's offense was slow to start, totaling only 17 yards and one first down in the first quarter.

In the second quarter, the Wolverines found their rhythm. Running back Blake Corum scored two one-yard rushing touchdowns, and quarterback J.J. McCarthy connected with wide receiver Roman Wilson for a two-yard touchdown pass, giving Michigan a 21-7 lead at halftime.

The Wolverines continued their dominance in the second half. McCarthy threw a 54-yard touchdown pass to tight end Colston Loveland and a seven-yard touchdown pass to wide receiver Semaj Morgan. The defense contributed with turnovers, including interceptions by Rod Moore and Keon Sabb, and fumble recoveries by Michael Barrett and Mason Graham. Backup quarterback Jack Tuttle added a four-yard touchdown pass to Karmello English, sealing the 52-7 victory.

Notable Performances

  • J.J. McCarthy: The junior quarterback completed 14 of 17 passes for 222 yards and three touchdowns, demonstrating efficiency and poise.
  • Blake Corum: The senior running back rushed for 52 yards and two touchdowns, surpassing 3,000 career rushing yards and moving to third in program history for career rushing touchdowns.
  • Defense: The unit forced four turnovers and held Indiana scoreless for the final 47 minutes of the game.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh praised the team's resilience after a slow start, highlighting the defense's ability to create scoring opportunities and the offense's balanced attack. The victory marked Michigan's 19th consecutive Big Ten win, tying a program record.
