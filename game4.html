Week 4: Rutgers @ Michigan

Date: September 23, 2023

Stadium: Michigan Stadium, Ann Arbor, MI

Result: W 31-7

Pre-Game Storylines

This game marked the return of head coach Jim Harbaugh to the sidelines after serving a three-game suspension. The Wolverines aimed to maintain their undefeated record and begin Big Ten Conference play on a strong note.

Game Recap

Rutgers struck first with a 69-yard touchdown pass from quarterback Gavin Wimsatt to wide receiver Christian Dremel, giving the Scarlet Knights an early 7-0 lead. This marked the first time Michigan trailed during the 2023 season. The Wolverines responded with a two-yard touchdown run by Blake Corum, tying the game at 7-7.

In the second quarter, quarterback J.J. McCarthy connected with wide receiver Semaj Morgan for an 18-yard touchdown pass, putting Michigan ahead 14-7 at halftime.

The Wolverines extended their lead in the third quarter with a 46-yard field goal by James Turner. Defensive back Mike Sainristil then intercepted a pass and returned it 71 yards for a touchdown, making the score 24-7.

In the fourth quarter, Corum added a five-yard touchdown run, sealing the 31-7 victory for Michigan.

Notable Performances

  • Blake Corum: The running back rushed for 97 yards and two touchdowns on 21 carries, showcasing his consistency and playmaking ability.
  • J.J. McCarthy: The quarterback completed 15 of 21 passes for 214 yards and one touchdown, effectively managing the offense.
  • Mike Sainristil: The defensive back's 71-yard interception return for a touchdown was a pivotal play that shifted momentum in Michigan's favor.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh expressed satisfaction with the team's resilience after an early deficit and praised the defense for forcing key turnovers. The victory marked Harbaugh's 75th at Michigan, making him the fourth coach in program history to reach this milestone.
