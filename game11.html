Week 11: Michigan @ Maryland

Date: November 18, 2023

Stadium: SECU Stadium, College Park, MD

Result: W 31-24

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, aiming to maintain their undefeated record and secure the program's 1,000th victory. The team continued to navigate challenges amid an ongoing NCAA investigation into alleged sign-stealing, with head coach Jim Harbaugh serving the second game of a suspension. Offensive coordinator Sherrone Moore assumed head coaching duties for the second consecutive week.

Game Recap

The Wolverines established an early lead, with running back Blake Corum scoring two touchdowns in the first half. Defensive back Mike Sainristil contributed significantly with two interceptions, one of which set up a scoring drive. Michigan's defense held strong, limiting Maryland's offensive production and securing the win despite a late push from the Terrapins.

Notable Performances

  • Blake Corum: The senior running back rushed for two touchdowns, contributing to Michigan's early lead.
  • Mike Sainristil: The defensive back recorded two interceptions, playing a pivotal role in stifling Maryland's offense.
  • J.J. McCarthy: The quarterback managed the game effectively, leading scoring drives and maintaining composure under pressure.

Post-Game Remarks

Acting head coach Sherrone Moore praised the team's resilience and focus amid external challenges, highlighting the defense's critical plays and the offense's ability to capitalize on opportunities. The victory marked a historic milestone, making Michigan the first college football program to achieve 1,000 wins.
