Game 15: The National Championship

Date: January 8, 2024

Stadium: NRG Stadium, Houston, TX

Result: W 34-13

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game as the No. 1 seed, aiming for its first national championship since 1997. Washington, the No. 2 seed, sought its first national title since 1991. The matchup featured two of the nation's top offenses, with Michigan's ground game and Washington's aerial attack under scrutiny.

Game Recap

Michigan struck first with a 41-yard touchdown run by Donovan Edwards. Washington responded with a 25-yard field goal, making it 7-3. Edwards added a 46-yard touchdown run, extending the lead to 14-3. A 31-yard field goal by James Turner increased Michigan's advantage to 17-3. Washington narrowed the gap with a 3-yard touchdown pass from Michael Penix Jr. to Jalen McMillan, ending the half at 17-10.

In the third quarter, Turner kicked a 38-yard field goal, making it 20-10. Washington answered with a 45-yard field goal, bringing the score to 20-13. In the fourth quarter, Blake Corum scored on a 12-yard run, and following an interception by Mike Sainristil, added a 1-yard touchdown, sealing the 34-13 victory.

Notable Performances

  • Donovan Edwards: Rushed for 104 yards and two touchdowns on six carries.
  • Blake Corum: Added 134 yards and two touchdowns on 21 carries.
  • Mike Sainristil: Recorded a pivotal interception in the fourth quarter.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh celebrated the culmination of a three-year journey, stating, "Business is finished." The victory marked Michigan's first national championship in the College Football Playoff era.
