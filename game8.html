Week 8: Michigan @ Michigan State

Date: October 21, 2023

Stadium: Spartan Stadium, East Lansing, MI

Result: W 49-0

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, aiming to maintain their undefeated record and retain the Paul Bunyan Trophy. The matchup was overshadowed by allegations of sign-stealing against Michigan, leading to increased scrutiny and adjustments in gameplay strategies.

Game Recap

The Wolverines established dominance early, with running back Blake Corum scoring a one-yard touchdown run in the first quarter. Quarterback J.J. McCarthy showcased exceptional performance, completing 21 of 27 passes for 287 yards and four touchdowns. He connected with wide receivers Roman Wilson and Colston Loveland for multiple scores, contributing to a 28-0 lead by halftime.

The defense was formidable, with defensive back Mike Sainristil returning an interception 72 yards for a touchdown in the third quarter. Michigan's offense continued to excel, with tight end A.J. Barner and quarterback Alex Orji adding touchdowns, culminating in a 49-0 shutout.

Notable Performances

  • J.J. McCarthy: The junior quarterback threw for 287 yards and four touchdowns, demonstrating precision and leadership.
  • Blake Corum: The senior running back contributed with a rushing touchdown, reinforcing his role as a key offensive player.
  • Mike Sainristil: The senior defensive back's interception return for a touchdown highlighted the defense's aggressive play.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh commended the team's comprehensive performance, emphasizing the offense's efficiency and the defense's resilience amid external distractions. The victory marked Michigan's largest margin over Michigan State since 1947 and their first shutout against the Spartans since 2000.
