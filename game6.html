Week 6: Michigan @ Minnesota

Date: October 7, 2023

Stadium: Huntington Bank Stadium, Minneapolis, MN

Result: W 52-10

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, aiming to maintain their undefeated record. Minnesota sought to challenge the Wolverines and claim the historic rivalry trophy, the Little Brown Jug.

Game Recap

The Wolverines set the tone early, with cornerback Will Johnson intercepting a pass on Minnesota's second play and returning it 36 yards for a touchdown. Quarterback J.J. McCarthy showcased his versatility, completing 14 of 20 passes for 219 yards and a touchdown, while also rushing for two scores. The defense dominated, with safety Keon Sabb adding a 28-yard interception return for a touchdown. Michigan's offense was balanced, accumulating 432 total yards, including 191 rushing yards. Minnesota's lone touchdown came late in the first half on a 35-yard pass from Athan Kaliakmanis to Daniel Jackson.

Notable Performances

  • J.J. McCarthy: The junior quarterback accounted for three touchdowns—two rushing and one passing—demonstrating effective leadership.
  • Will Johnson: The sophomore cornerback's early pick-six set the defensive tone for the game.
  • Keon Sabb: The sophomore safety's interception return for a touchdown further solidified the defense's dominance.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh praised the team's comprehensive performance, highlighting the defense's ability to create scoring opportunities. Minnesota's head coach P.J. Fleck commended Michigan, stating, "They're one of the deepest teams, one of the best teams, one of the biggest teams, fastest teams, strongest teams, and they do not make mistakes."
