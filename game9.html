Week 9: Purdue @ Michigan

Date: November 4, 2023

Stadium: Michigan Stadium, Ann Arbor, MI

Result: W 41-13

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, aiming to maintain their undefeated record amid external scrutiny due to an ongoing NCAA investigation into alleged sign-stealing. Purdue, under head coach Ryan Walters, sought to challenge the Wolverines by implementing new signal-calling strategies in response to the allegations.

Game Recap

The Wolverines established dominance early, with running back Blake Corum scoring two rushing touchdowns in the first quarter. Quarterback J.J. McCarthy showcased his passing prowess, completing 24 of 37 passes for 335 yards. The defense was formidable, limiting Purdue to six points until a late touchdown in the final seconds.

Notable Performances

  • Blake Corum: The senior running back rushed for three touchdowns, bringing his season total to 16 and tying him for second all-time in Michigan history with 47 rushing TDs.
  • J.J. McCarthy: The junior quarterback threw for 335 yards, marking his second-highest career total in passing yards.
  • Roman Wilson: The wide receiver recorded career highs with nine receptions for 143 yards.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh praised the team's focus and execution amid external distractions, highlighting the offense's efficiency and the defense's resilience. The victory extended Michigan's program record of scoring 40-plus points to five consecutive Big Ten games.
