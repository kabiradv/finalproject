Week 5: Michigan @ Nebraska

Date: September 30, 2023

Stadium: Memorial Stadium, Lincoln, NE

Result: W 45-7

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, aiming to maintain their undefeated record in their first road test of the season. Nebraska, under new head coach Matt Rhule, sought to leverage home-field advantage to challenge the Wolverines.

Game Recap

The Wolverines set the tone early, orchestrating a 75-yard opening drive capped by a 29-yard touchdown pass from quarterback J.J. McCarthy to wide receiver Roman Wilson. On Nebraska's ensuing possession, defensive lineman Kenneth Grant intercepted a tipped pass, leading to a 20-yard touchdown run by running back Kalel Mullings, putting Michigan ahead 14-0.

In the second quarter, McCarthy showcased his dual-threat capability with a 21-yard touchdown scramble. He later connected with Wilson again for a 16-yard touchdown pass, extending the lead to 28-0 by halftime.

The Wolverines continued their dominance in the second half. Running back Blake Corum added a one-yard touchdown run in the third quarter, and kicker James Turner converted a 30-yard field goal early in the fourth. Backup quarterback Jayden Denegal threw his first career touchdown pass, an eight-yard strike to wide receiver Peyton O'Leary, making it 45-0. Nebraska's sole score came late in the fourth quarter on a 74-yard touchdown run by Joshua Fleeks.

Notable Performances

  • J.J. McCarthy: The junior quarterback completed 12 of 16 passes for 156 yards and two touchdowns, adding a 21-yard rushing touchdown.
  • Roman Wilson: The senior wide receiver recorded four receptions for 58 yards and two touchdowns, bringing his season total to nine touchdown catches.
  • Defense: The unit forced multiple turnovers, including interceptions by Kenneth Grant and others, and held Nebraska to just seven points.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh praised the team's comprehensive performance, highlighting the defense's ability to create turnovers and the offense's efficiency. The victory improved Michigan's record to 5-0, reinforcing their status as a top contender in the Big Ten.
