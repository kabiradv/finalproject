Game 13: Big Ten Championship

Date: December 2, 2023

Stadium: Lucas Oil Stadium, Indianapolis, IN

Result: W 26-0

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, aiming for their third consecutive Big Ten title and a likely top seed in the College Football Playoff. Head coach Jim Harbaugh returned to the sideline after serving a suspension, bringing stability to the team. Iowa, ranked No. 18, sought to challenge Michigan's dominance with their strong defense.

Game Recap

The Wolverines took an early lead with a 35-yard field goal by James Turner. Following a remarkable 82-yard punt return by Semaj Morgan to Iowa's five-yard line, Blake Corum scored a two-yard touchdown run, making it 10-0 at the end of the first quarter.

The second quarter was a defensive battle, with neither team scoring, leaving Michigan ahead 10-0 at halftime.

In the third quarter, Michigan's defense intensified. Mike Sainristil forced a fumble recovered at Iowa's six-yard line, leading to Corum's six-yard touchdown run, tying him with Anthony Thomas for Michigan's modern-era record of 55 rushing touchdowns. Turner added a 46-yard field goal, extending the lead to 20-0.

The defense continued to dominate in the fourth quarter, with Braiden McGregor forcing another fumble recovered at Iowa's 15-yard line. Turner kicked two more field goals, including a Big Ten Championship Game record 50-yarder, sealing the 26-0 victory.

Notable Performances

  • Blake Corum: The senior running back rushed for two touchdowns, tying Michigan's modern-era record with 55 career rushing TDs.
  • Mike Sainristil: The defensive back forced two fumbles and recorded a sack, earning the game's MVP honors.
  • Semaj Morgan: His 82-yard punt return set up a crucial touchdown and marked the longest in Big Ten Championship Game history.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh praised the team's resilience and focus, highlighting the defense's exceptional performance in achieving the shutout. The victory marked Michigan's third consecutive Big Ten title, a first in program history, and solidified their position for a top seed in the College Football Playoff.
