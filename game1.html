Week 1: East Carolina @ Michigan

Date: September 2, 2023

Stadium: Michigan Stadium, Ann Arbor, MI

Result: W 30-3

Pre-Game Storylines

The Wolverines entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally, facing the challenge of playing without head coach Jim Harbaugh, who was serving a three-game suspension. Defensive coordinator Jesse Minter stepped in as acting head coach for this game. Additionally, there were concerns regarding the availability of key players due to injuries, including Rod Moore, Makari Paige, Tyler Morris, and Amorion Walker.

Game Recap

Michigan's defense set the tone early, forcing a three-and-out on East Carolina's opening drive. The Wolverines capitalized on their second possession with a 14-yard touchdown pass from quarterback J.J. McCarthy to wide receiver Roman Wilson. This duo connected again in the second quarter for a 10-yard touchdown, contributing to a 23-0 halftime lead.

In the third quarter, McCarthy and Wilson continued their synergy with a 15-yard touchdown pass, marking their third scoring connection of the game. East Carolina avoided a shutout with a 33-yard field goal by Andrew Conrad as time expired, concluding the game at 30-3.

Notable Performances

  • J.J. McCarthy: The junior quarterback showcased precision, completing 26 of 30 passes for 280 yards and three touchdowns. His 86.7% completion rate stands as the second-highest in Michigan's program history.
  • Roman Wilson: The senior wide receiver was McCarthy's primary target, recording six receptions for 78 yards and three touchdowns.
  • Blake Corum: Returning from injury, the running back rushed for 73 yards on 10 carries, including a two-yard touchdown run in the second quarter.

Defensive Highlights

Michigan's defense stifled East Carolina's offense, limiting them to just 235 total yards and forcing a crucial interception in the first quarter. The defensive unit maintained pressure throughout the game, contributing significantly to the lopsided score.

Post-Game Remarks

Acting head coach Jesse Minter expressed pride in the team's performance, emphasizing their focus and execution despite the absence of Coach Harbaugh. The victory set a positive tone for the Wolverines' season, highlighting their depth and resilience.
