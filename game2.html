Week 2: UNLV @ Michigan

Date: September 9, 2023

Stadium: Michigan Stadium, Ann Arbor, MI

Result: W 35-7

Pre-Game Storylines

The Wolverines entered the game ranked No. 2 nationally and continued to navigate the absence of head coach Jim Harbaugh, who was serving the second game of a three-game suspension. In a unique arrangement, special teams coordinator Jay Harbaugh served as acting head coach during the first half, while running backs coach Mike Hart took over in the second half, marking the first time an African American served in the head coaching role for Michigan football.

Game Recap

Michigan's offense set an assertive tone from the outset. On their opening drive, the Wolverines marched 80 yards in nine plays, culminating in a three-yard touchdown run by Blake Corum. The momentum continued into the second quarter, with Corum adding a two-yard touchdown run, extending the lead to 14-0.

Quarterback J.J. McCarthy showcased exceptional accuracy, completing 22 of 25 passes for 278 yards and two touchdowns. His primary target, Roman Wilson, hauled in two touchdown receptions, including a notable 47-yard catch in the third quarter.

Defensively, Michigan stifled UNLV's offense, limiting them to a single touchdown late in the fourth quarter. The Wolverines' defense applied consistent pressure, recording multiple sacks and forcing key stops throughout the game.

Notable Performances

  • Blake Corum: The senior running back rushed for 80 yards on 15 carries, scoring three touchdowns.
  • Roman Wilson: The wide receiver continued his strong start to the season with two touchdown receptions, further establishing himself as a key offensive weapon.
  • Defense: The unit held UNLV to just seven points, showcasing depth and resilience despite missing key players due to injury.

Post-Game Remarks

Acting head coaches Jay Harbaugh and Mike Hart expressed satisfaction with the team's performance, highlighting the players' focus and execution amid coaching adjustments. The victory extended Michigan's home winning streak to 17 games, the longest under Coach Harbaugh's tenure.
