Game 14: The Rose Bowl

Date: January 1, 2024

Stadium: The Rose Bowl, Pasadena, CA

Result: W 27-20 (OT)

Pre-Game Storylines

Michigan entered the game as the top seed, aiming for its first national championship appearance in the playoff era. Alabama, seeded fourth, sought to return to the title game after a one-year absence. The matchup featured two storied programs with a combined 25 national titles, adding to the anticipation.

Game Recap

The game began with both teams exchanging touchdowns in the first quarter: Alabama's Jase McClellan scored on a short run, and Michigan's J.J. McCarthy connected with Roman Wilson for a 25-yard touchdown pass. In the second quarter, Michigan took a 13-10 lead into halftime, courtesy of two field goals by James Turner.

The third quarter was scoreless, marked by strong defensive play from both sides. In the fourth quarter, Alabama's Will Reichard kicked a field goal to tie the game at 13-13. Michigan responded with a 10-yard touchdown run by Blake Corum, but Alabama's Jalen Milroe threw a touchdown pass to Jermaine Burton, tying the game at 20-20 and forcing overtime.

In overtime, Michigan's offense struck quickly. On the second play, Corum broke free for a 17-yard touchdown run, giving the Wolverines a 27-20 lead. Alabama's ensuing possession ended on a fourth-down stop by Michigan's defense, sealing the victory.

Notable Performances

  • Blake Corum: The senior running back rushed for 83 yards and two touchdowns, including the game-winner in overtime.
  • J.J. McCarthy: The junior quarterback completed 17 of 27 passes for 221 yards and a touchdown, managing the offense effectively.
  • Mason Graham: The defensive tackle recorded two sacks and was named Defensive Player of the Game.

Post-Game Remarks

Head coach Jim Harbaugh praised his team's resilience and composure, stating, "Our guys showed tremendous heart and determination. This was a total team effort." The victory advanced Michigan to its first College Football Playoff National Championship game.
